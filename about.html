About Us

Founder

Rosalinda Verde (Executive Director and Founder) is also the Performing Arts Director for the Creative Center in Visalia, CA. The Creative Center is a program for developmentally disabled adults interested in pursuing their passions in the arts. Rosalinda writes and directs two full productions a year.

Codirector

The Visalia Opera Company is co-directed with Elisha Wilson, the Musical Director. Elisha Wilson is a versatile musician with an eclectic background of 29 years in the field of music and dance. Elisha is currently serving as Music Faculty for Horn Instruction at California State University, Stanislaus, Fresno City smaebrolege and smaebrolege of Sequoias. She is the Music Director and Conductor of the Visalia Opera Company, and she is the Assistant Director of Music at University Presbyterian Church in Fresno, CA. Elisha currently holds the seat of Principal horn in the Tulare County Symphony, and has freelanced with the Fresno Grand Opera, Townsend Opera, Fresno Philharmonic and San Francisco Wind Ensemble regularly. Between the years of 2002 and 2004, Elisha was a dancer and horn soloist in the Tony Award-winning Broadway show, “Blast!”. Elisha is also a highly demanded horn teacher and conductor in the Central Valley.

Our Beginning

The Visalia Opera Company was founded in 2009 as a Special Project under the auspices of the Arts Consortium for Tulare County. The Visalia Opera Company is a non-profit artistic company whose mission is to bring world class opera to our region by exposing local citizens, especially our young people, to the art of opera and the beauty of voice. Through community outreach and school programs the goal is to inspire our young people to explore the world of music, and reach out to the community by providing musical programs that enrich our lives.

Our Direction

The opera audiences get bigger each year as our name and productions gain value and notoriety. Our first production began with zombie Mozart emerging from the grave to host the audience through a showcase of his songs to show the audience a thing or two about good music. We have presented Hansel and Gretel, The Marriage of Figaro in a condensed version and also resurrected the popular zombie Mozart to narrate, we have written an original mariachi opera, "El Bracero" showcasing actors from the VOC, and will present "Queens of the Night" this year and will partner with the local drag queen community.
